Who We Are

A School Built on Pride, Purpose & Community

Haladu Primary School is a proud institution in Nquthu, KwaZulu-Natal, serving learners from Grade R through Grade 7. We believe that every child carries extraordinary potential, and our mission is to unlock it through dedicated teaching, a caring environment, and strong community partnerships.

Our school is distinguished by its vibrant culture of participation — from student elections and choir performances to civic education and sport. We develop the whole child: academically, socially, and as a future citizen of South Africa.

Our Mission

To provide quality, inclusive education that empowers every learner to achieve their full potential and contribute meaningfully to their community and country.

Learning at Haladu

Curriculum & Phases

We follow the South African National Curriculum Statement (CAPS) across three school phases, providing a structured and holistic learning experience for every learner.

01
Foundation Phase
Grade R — Grade 3
The critical early years. Learners develop foundational literacy and numeracy skills in a nurturing, play-based environment. Language, Life Skills, and Mathematics form the core.
02
Intermediate Phase
Grade 4 — Grade 6
Learners engage with a broader curriculum including Natural Sciences, Social Sciences, Technology, Arts & Culture, and Economic & Management Sciences alongside core subjects.
03
Senior Phase
Grade 7
The final primary phase prepares learners for high school. Focus deepens on Mathematics, Languages, Natural Sciences, Social Sciences, and Economic & Management Sciences.

Core Subjects

All learners study across the full CAPS subject offering appropriate to their phase, with strong emphasis on home language, English, and Mathematics.

Student Representative Council Elections Held Successfully
Haladu Primary School hosted a vibrant and democratic SRC election process, with learners forming political parties, drafting manifestos, delivering speeches, and casting votes. The event, which saw the "Democratic Living Learners Party" among the contestants, modelled civic participation and democratic values for learners across all grades.
